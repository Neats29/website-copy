A list of useful applications and websites:

Here is my list of recommended services with a focus on privacy. Since the leak of the NSA documents by the wistleblower Edward Snowden I have reflected on my internet activities and have sought services that value privacy and encyptions. However not everything on this list is about privacy, some of these are just my favourite tools (in no specific order).

    • Duck Duck Go as an alternative

    I admit this search engine is no where as good as Google but this is a search engine that doesn't track your seraches, so if you have an embaressing serach like that itchy elbow or something private you may want to use DDG and you might just start using it more.

    • Drop Dropbox and use Spideroak

    This is a cloud service that values your privacy (unlike many other services), you get 2GB of free storage to start with

    • Reveal the ghosts creaping into your browser

    This is a free plug-in you can very easily download on your browser, it shows you all the companies tracking you like Google analytics and you can choose to block them.

    • Ditch Gmail & pick up ProtonMail

    Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook etc are all unsafe email services. If you don't want your emails to be read, request an invite to get an account on ProtonMail. This is a service that started as a direct result of Snowden's leaks, a couple of smart kids from MIT and CERN came up with a form of encyption that means even the company itself has no access to your data, the servers are also based at Switzerland, a neutral state independent of the EU and US. They have since seen a great demand and are in the process of purchasing more servers so I am still on the waiting list!

    • Learn to code on Treehouse.

    When I first decided to learn coding I tried Codeacademy, and while it was a good place to start, it was hard to apply the knowledge I gained. Soon after I discovered Treehouse, and I have been hooked since! It is an amazing and affordable online learning resource. It is one of the most enjoyable learning experiences I have had.

    • Adobe Brackets, an awesome Text Editor

    I may be new to coding but I enjoy testing different tools before I choose one and coding was no different. I have tried Sublime Text, Atom and Brackets so could only compare these but Brackets is definitly my favourite. It is free and open source, installing plugins and themes is super easy and overall it is a seamless experience. GitHub's Atom is also free and open source and comes very close.

    • Steller, showcasing humanity

    If you are feeling cynical, just have a peak around Steller. You will regain faith in humanity and fall involve with the planet. Some of the most talented and adventurous photographers share their work on here. This is predominently an iPhone app but they also have a website.

    • F.lux for nighttime surfing

    If you surf the internet or use your computer in the evenings, you may want to download f.lux, it is a free software that is supposed to reduce eye strain and sleep disruption by matching your screen colour temperature to the time of the day. It takes some getting used to but since downloading it, I've been using it everyday.

    • Logic Pro: The best DAW!

    Logic Pro is a digital audio workstation. It is hands down the best music production software by far (Ok I might be a bit biased) but it is an industry standard, the only downside is that it is exclusive to Apple and well the price tag could improve a bit.
